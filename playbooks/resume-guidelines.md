## Resume guidelines

### General guidelines
Standard structure - **NO FANCY TEMPLATES**.
Filename - `Name_Surname_Grade_Position.pdf`
Don't mention any potentially repelling points:
- age
- salary
- location
Use specific examples instead of generic adjectives: `leadership qualities` -> `mentored juniors`, etc.

### Structure
Blocks:
1. Header
2. About
3. Skills
4. Experience
5. Education
6. Courses, public speaking etc. (optional)

### 1. Header

**Name** is a hyperlink to LinkedIn
**Specific job title** - `Backend Developer`, `Full Stack Developer`, etc. + years of experience (5+)
**Email** - no transliteration, funny words, year of birth, russian/obscure mailboxes - e.g. `name-surname@gmail.com`
**Specify** phone number - EU, US, 'legit' country. Adds legitimacy to the resume, plus recruiters might write in whatsapp - **Airalo**, **Magti**, **Birka**
**Github** - a clickable url
**Photo** - only if handsome, **only** for RU resume 

**Don't** specify location - mention timezone instead
**Don't** specify age - ageism is real

All hyperlinks are **styled**, obvious they are clickable

### 2. About

The essence of experience - biggest achievements, written in non-technical language
2 paragraphs tops
**NO** - `I worked with technology X` - there are other places for the tech
**YES** - I worked with the full production cycle, agile, scrum, etc. releasing the software, responsible for the whole thing
Depending on the specialization:
- backend: highload, increased throughput, meddled in devops
- frontend: improved app load time, improved UX, etc
Demonstrate that I am a **T-shaped** professional - additionally to the main spec, I am interested in side stuff
Mention that I can work in small teams (even a one-man show), and large distributed teams
4 sentences max

#### 3. Skills

This is a cloud tag for ATS and recruiter - mention the most important stuff, in descending order of importance. Add old tech too (but lower in precedence) - might click with the recruiter.

Also include **architecture patterns** and **data structures**, popular libraries. Don't include obscure unpopular libs (even if they are very nice to use lol)

If out of space - include tags with size 1 and in white font (for ATS). If pdf - add tags/words to metadata

#### 4. Work experience

Same blocks for every place of work - `Company - Product (link) - month year started - month year finished - tenure in years`
Only specify companies with a client-facing product. If worked for a company without one - change to another company. Can even mention you worked at FAANG (then say that worked as a contractor for example - no one will check)

Can check list of eligible companies at integrator websites (EPAM etc) - they post what comanies they contract people out to

Responsibilities vs Achievements - **focus on achievements**. Don't mention cool/complex products - mention great achievements

Write facts as bullet points. 3-5 bullets per place of work, if more - move to the next company. Every bullet point should include: 
- What I did
- Why I did it
- What it achieved with **numbers**. **Don't** use even numbers, prefer decimals (increased performance by 15.5%).

**Red flags**:
Was responsible for X
Implemented X
Participated in X

**Green flags:**
Sped up by X
Increased by Y
Earned/saved Z

Every place of work more than 1 year. Don't go into details for places more than 5 years ago - just mention product, company, position, tenure

#### 5. Education

Mention masters degree in a russian university. Name of specialization should sound IT-ey
Mention only high-profile cetrifications (AWS, Google cloud, etc), no small courses
Mention teaching experience, open source contributions
