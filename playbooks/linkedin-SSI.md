# LinkedIn SSI Playbook

## Goal

- Main goal - rise to the top of *Sales Navigator* (Linkedin's AI)
- Optimize LinkedIn for inbound recruiter outreach, not for applying to jobs manually.
- `linkedin.com/sales/ssi` - check frequently, changes are reflected in 3 days. Target rating - **60+**
- Use profile analytics too, especially profile views, search appearances, and post impressions.

## SSI

- Aim to get above your market baseline first; the speaker saw weak inbound at around `35` SSI and much stronger inbound above `50`.
- Treat the four SSI buckets differently: profile completeness matters most once, content and relationships need ongoing maintenance, and advanced search matters less if time is limited.
- Expect delayed feedback. SSI changes can take a few days to update after profile edits.

## Profile

- Fill as many profile sections as possible. Easy wins include headline, about, experience, education, top skills, languages, certifications, projects, recommendations, and other optional sections.
- Write a headline that explains who you are immediately, without forcing a recruiter to scroll.
- Set country and city to the market where recruiters are likely to search for your role.
- Turn on `Open to Work` and configure target roles, work mode, and start availability.
- Use the `About` section as a short positioning summary: who you are, what hard problems you solve, and how to contact you.
- Add stack keywords for external parsers, but make the visible profile readable for humans first.
- Fill `Top Skills`. The speaker treats this as one of the strongest search fields.

## Experience

- Describe work in achievements, not duties.
- Prefer bullets like “built X, improved Y by Z%” over “worked on X” or “implemented Y”.
- Keep role descriptions short and outcome-focused.
- List relevant skills in experience entries, but assume only the first few matter for search.

## Search

- Use advanced search when building your network, especially early on.
- Add `10-15` targeted connections per day until you cross `500+` total connections.
- Treat `500+` as a practical threshold because it improves how your network size is displayed and signals account maturity.
- Prefer connection requests with a short note if the relationship itself matters. The transcript claims acceptance and conversation rates are better with notes.
- If time is tight, cut back on manual outreach before cutting profile completeness or content activity.

## Content

- Post consistently, but not too often. The transcript recommends about once per week.
- Optimize for interaction, not for perfect thought leadership. Likes, comments, and reposts are what move this SSI bucket.
- Comment on other people’s posts and keep lightweight engagement active between your own posts.
- Even a simple post can drive recruiter visits if it gets enough early engagement.
- Use AI to draft post text if speed matters, then edit for credibility.

## Network

- Keep strong links with people from previous jobs, school, and existing professional circles.
- This SSI bucket is not just about collecting contacts; it rewards visible relationship depth and interaction.
- Be careful with large company pages. The speaker saw a sharp SSI drop after linking a current role to a very large company where he had few real connections.
- If recruiters are unlikely to search by your school or employer page, the transcript argues that linking those cards may be less important than preserving SSI.

## Routine

- [x] First week: complete the profile, set market location, enable `Open to Work`, and define top skills.
- [x] Next `4-6` weeks: add `10-15` relevant connections per day until you pass `500+`.
- [ ] From the start: publish one post per week and actively drive early likes and comments.
- [ ] Review SSI and profile analytics every few days instead of reacting daily.

## Traps

- Do not rely on SSI alone. Market demand, geography, and role type still determine whether recruiters are searching for someone like you.
- Do not waste time over-optimizing advanced search if it produces little visible lift for your case.
- Do not write vague responsibility lists in experience sections.
- Do not pick a location that recruiters for your target market are unlikely to use.
- The transcript includes advice about using fake or non-linked company/school entries to influence SSI. Treat that as the speaker's tactic, not a neutral recommendation, because it can reduce trust and searchability in other filters.
