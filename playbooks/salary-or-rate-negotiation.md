# Salary Or Rate Negotiation

## Goal

- avoid anchoring too early
- learn the employer's budget first
- keep the conversation moving without giving away leverage

## Rule

- If a recruiter asks for salary expectations or hourly rate, do not give a number first.
- Ask for their budgeted range or compensation band instead.
- If needed, say that your expectations depend on scope, contract type, seniority, and total package.

## Short Patterns

```text
Regarding rate, I'd prefer to first understand the range you have budgeted for the position and the contract setup.
```

```text
In terms of compensation, I'd like to first understand the budgeted range for the position.
```

```text
My expectations depend quite a bit on the role scope, contract type, and total package. Could you share the range you have in mind?
```

## Avoid

- do not volunteer a number first
- do not say you are flexible without asking for their range
- do not turn the answer into a long negotiation speech
